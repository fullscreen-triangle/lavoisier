\subsection{Validation Direction 5: Outside-In (Thermodynamic Consistency)}
\label{sec:validation_outside_in}

\subsubsection{Categorical Thermodynamics}

The categorical temperature is defined as:
\begin{equation}
T_{\text{cat}} = \frac{\hbar}{\kB} \frac{dM}{dt}
\end{equation}
where $M$ is the number of categorical states traversed per unit time.

For a molecular vibration with frequency $\nu$ and $N_{\text{cat}}$ categorical states per period:
\begin{equation}
\frac{dM}{dt} = \nu \cdot N_{\text{cat}}
\end{equation}

Therefore:
\begin{equation}
T_{\text{cat}} = \frac{\hbar}{\kB} \nu N_{\text{cat}}
\end{equation}

The vibrational temperature is:
\begin{equation}
T_{\text{vib}} = \frac{h\nu}{\kB}
\end{equation}

The ratio is:
\begin{equation}
\frac{T_{\text{cat}}}{T_{\text{vib}}} = \frac{\hbar \nu N_{\text{cat}}}{h\nu} = \frac{N_{\text{cat}}}{2\pi}
\end{equation}

For $N_{\text{cat}} \sim 10^{52}$:
\begin{equation}
T_{\text{cat}} \sim \frac{10^{52}}{2\pi} T_{\text{vib}} \sim 1.6 \times 10^{51} T_{\text{vib}}
\end{equation}
